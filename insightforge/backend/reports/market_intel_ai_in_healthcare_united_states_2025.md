## 5. Market Trends Section
### 5.1 Major Trends
| Trigger | Scenario Type | Impact | Examples (3-5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical workforce shortages | Demand | Accelerates automation and decision support purchases | AI triage, Ambient documentation, Coding automation |
| Foundation model maturity | Technology | Expands use-cases from pilots to enterprise workflows | Multimodal imaging AI, Clinical copilots, Personalized patient communication |
| Value-based care pressures | Commercial | Strengthens ROI focus on risk prediction and intervention | Readmission reduction, Care gap detection, Population stratification |

### 5.2 Key Drivers
| Trigger | Scenario Type | Impact | Examples (3-5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Policy push for interoperability | Regulatory | Improves data access needed for model deployment | FHIR APIs, TEFCA expansion, Data-sharing mandates |
| Cloud modernization | Supply | Lowers infrastructure barriers for AI at scale | Managed ML platforms, MLOps tooling, Secure data lakes |
| Payer demand for efficiency | Demand | Boosts adoption in claims and utilization management | Claims automation, Fraud detection, Prior-auth optimization |

### 5.3 Key Barriers
| Trigger | Scenario Type | Impact | Examples (3-5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regulatory uncertainty for high-risk AI | Regulatory | Slows procurement in clinical-grade applications | Model transparency requirements, Post-market monitoring, Validation burden |
| Data quality fragmentation | Supply | Reduces model portability and performance consistency | Inconsistent coding, Missing longitudinal data, Siloed records |
| Cybersecurity and privacy exposure | Macro (VUCA) | Raises compliance and deployment costs | Ransomware risk, Third-party model governance, PHI leakage controls |
